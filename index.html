Detail of tourist sites

Grand Canyon: One of the most famous natural attractions in the United States, the Grand Canyon is a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in Arizona. Known for its visually overwhelming size and intricate landscape, the Grand Canyon attracts millions of visitors each year, offering stunning panoramic views and numerous recreational opportunities.

Machu Picchu: Located in Peru, Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city set high in the Andes Mountains. It's renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments, and panoramic views. This UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the most iconic and visited archaeological sites in the world.

Eiffel Tower: A symbol of Paris and one of the most recognizable structures in the world, the Eiffel Tower attracts millions of visitors annually. Completed in 1889, it was initially criticized by some of France's leading artists and intellectuals for its design but has since become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world.
